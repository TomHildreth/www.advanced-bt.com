WILDFIRE HOME PROTECTION
WallGuard (and RoofGuard) is formulated with 3M ceramic for thermal and fire resistance. According to Intertek's fire lab WallGuard is the only spray-on exterior coating they have tested that passed the ASTM E2768 30-minute test (see report summary below) with flames directly against WallGuard that earned the highest Class-A Fire Rating with ZERO flame and smoke spread (comparable to asbestos).
Why some L.A. homes remain standing next to ones destroyed by fires: "Like a blow torch" by CBS News Jan. 20, 2025. Stucco (a cementacious material) is a popular exterior finish because homeowners' belief that it is fire-resistant, but all cementacious exterior finishes of stucco, fiber cement board (FCB) and FCB lap siding have near zero thermal resistance to wildfire's high heat. According to the Federal Oak Ridge Tennessee Lab test report, tilt-up concrete walls (and therefore FCB, lap siding, and stucco) have a Whole-Wall R-Value of 0.6, i.e. near zero thermal resistance.
WallGuard is the 1st Choice for wildfire protection, because it reduces wildfire's high heat of over 1,000℉ surface temperature to 314℉ inside the walls (-69% thermal resistance), which protects the wood interior structure from reaching its 450℉ ignition temperature. Refer to Test 1 below
